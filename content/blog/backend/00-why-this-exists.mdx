I've been building things for a while now. Projects, systems, random experiments at 2am that probably didn't need to exist - you know how it goes.

And somewhere along the way, I started noticing a pattern. Every time I learned something properly - not just "oh I know what this is" but actually sat with it, broke it, fixed it, and could explain it to someone else - it stuck. Everything else kind of faded.

This blog is the result of that realization.

---

## What this is

This is a place where I write about things I'm learning, building, or thinking about - mostly around backend engineering, systems, and the craft of writing software that actually works in the real world.

Not tutorials. Not "10 things you need to know about X." Just honest writing about concepts, the why behind decisions, and things I wish someone had explained to me earlier.

Some posts will be long and detailed. Some will be short and direct. Depends on the topic.

---

## Who it's for

Honestly? Anyone who's curious.

If you're early in your journey and backend feels like a giant black box - this might help demystify some things. If you're already building stuff and want a different perspective on something - cool, let's think together.

I'm also running some internal sessions with my club juniors this summer, going through backend from the ground up. A lot of posts here will come directly from those sessions - notes, concepts, code references - so that there's something to come back to later.

But the writing isn't exclusive to that. It's for anyone who finds it useful.

---

## Why write at all

I could keep notes privately. I do, actually.

But there's something about writing for someone else that forces you to actually understand what you're saying. You can't hand-wave through a concept when you know someone's going to read it and go "wait, that doesn't make sense."

So this is part selfish - writing makes me sharper. And part genuine - if someone finds it useful, that's a good thing.

---

## What to expect

- Backend concepts explained properly - HTTP, databases, auth, caching, queues, scaling, and more
- Code references in **Node.js / TypeScript** and **Python (FastAPI)**
- Honest takes on how things work, why they're designed that way, and where people usually get confused
- Occasional posts on other things I'm building or thinking about

No fluff. No padding. Just the stuff that actually matters.

If something's unclear, wrong, or you just want to talk about it - reach out. I'm always up for a good conversation.

Let's get into it.
